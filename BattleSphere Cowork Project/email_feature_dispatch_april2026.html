BattleSphere Platform Dispatch
April 2026 Three new capabilities

Commander

The forge has been busy.
Three new capabilities are now live.

Since launch, you have asked for broader formats, richer events, and a way to document the armies that fight these wars. We listened. All three are now live on BattleSphere — and each one is available in every campaign today.

New — Format

League Campaign Management

Not every campaign needs a map and a frontline. Some groups prefer a structured competitive league — a fixed season of games, a clean standings table, and a single champion at the end. BattleSphere now supports both.

When you create a League campaign, BattleSphere replaces the map with a live league table: wins, draws, losses, and points, updated automatically as results are logged. Everything else — battle records, chronicles, player profiles, faction standing — carries over exactly as you know it.

"Create your campaign, choose League format, invite your players. The table builds itself from the first result you log."

View the demo league →

New — Narrative Campaigns

Dynamic Campaign Events

A campaign without stakes is just a scoreboard. Events are how the organiser makes every game matter. Post a campaign event — a sudden offensive, a warp storm, a desperate last stand — and define exactly what it means on the map.

Events can now carry mechanical weight. Attach a bonus that fires automatically when a qualifying battle is logged while the event is active — based on which territory was contested, which battle type was played, or which faction was involved. Stack conditions freely.

Go further with a Territory Cascade: designate a key world as the trigger. When a faction conquers it, the bonus cascades instantly to every star system connected by warp route — rewarding strategic ambition without manual bookkeeping.

"The Council has declared a Crusade. Every battle fought in the outer systems now carries double influence — and one key world will decide who holds the entire rimward flank."

— The kind of event you can now post in minutes.

See events in the Austriacus Subsector →

New — For Every Player

Army Portfolio

Campaigns are fought by armies, and armies deserve a record of their own. Every player on BattleSphere can now build a public Army Portfolio — a home for the force behind the battles, independent of any single campaign.

Add your units, upload photos, and write a portrait for each model. If you play Crusade-style campaigns, your unit's XP, battle honours, kills, and upgrades are tracked automatically and displayed right on the unit's page. Share a unit link and a rich preview card appears in Discord — photo, name, faction, and record.

Your Portfolio is yours to keep. The armies grow between campaigns; the record follows them wherever they fight next.

Your armies. Your miniatures. A permanent record — long after the campaign ends.

These three additions make BattleSphere something more flexible than it was at launch: a platform for any format your group plays, with tools for every kind of organiser and every kind of player. More is coming.

If you have been waiting to start a campaign — or restart one — now is a good time.
