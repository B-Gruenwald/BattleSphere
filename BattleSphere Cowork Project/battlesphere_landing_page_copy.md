# BattleSphere — Landing Page Copy
*Revised · April 2026*

---

## Design intent

The campaign organiser is the primary audience — they're the ones who create campaigns and bring their group in. But the experience is collective: the organiser sets the stage and shapes the narrative, and the players bring it to life through the battles they fight and the history they build together. Both matter. The copy should honour that.

## SECTION 2 — FEATURE BLOCKS

---

### Block 1: The Map
**Headline:** *Build the world. Watch it change.*

> Organise your campaign around a living map of territories and warp routes. As factions clash and control shifts, every player can see exactly where the war stands — and what it took to get there.

---

### Block 2: Campaign Events
**Headline:** *Give every game a bigger meaning.*

> Organisers can post campaign events — sudden assaults, shifting allegiances, sector-wide crises — that reframe what's at stake and make individual games matter beyond the final score. Players don't just show up to play. They show up to find out what happens next.

---

### Block 3: The Chronicle
**Headline:** *A history built by everyone.*

> Every battle logged, every event posted, every territory that changes hands — BattleSphere weaves it all into a living timeline. The Chronicle belongs to the whole group: a record of the war they fought together, told in the order it happened.

---

### Block 4: Honours & Achievements
**Headline:** *Legends are made here.*

> Organisers can bestow honours on players and factions for acts of brilliance, sacrifice, or infamy. Recorded permanently in the Hall of Honours, these moments become part of the campaign's shared legend — the stories your group will still be telling long after the campaign ends.

---

## SECTION 3 — THE PITCH
**Heading:** *More than a league. A living campaign.*

> Running a campaign means giving your group's games a context that makes them matter. BattleSphere handles the organisation — the map, the standings, the history — so you can focus on the story. And your players bring that story to life every time they roll dice.

---

## SECTION 4 — HOW IT WORKS

**Heading:** *Up and running in minutes*

1. **Create your campaign** — Name it, build your map, set up factions and territories.
2. **Invite your players** — Share your campaign link. Players join, log their battles, and watch the map evolve.
3. **Shape the narrative together** — Post events, award honours, and let the campaign's history write itself.

## Notes on tone

- The organiser **leads** — they create, invite, post events, award honours
- The players **bring it to life** — they fight battles, build the Chronicle, shape the outcome
- Both are honoured; neither overshadows the other
- Avoid comparisons to other tools — the value stands on its own
